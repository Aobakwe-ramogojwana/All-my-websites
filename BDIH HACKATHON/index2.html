Botswana Communications Regulatory Authority (BOCRA) was established through the Communications Regulatory Authority Act, 2012 (CRA Act) on the 1st of April 2013 to regulate the communications sector in Botswana, comprising telecommunications, Internet and ICTs, radio communications, broadcasting, postal services and related matters.

The CRA Act replaced the Broadcasting Act [Cap 72:04] and the Telecommunications Act [Cap 72:03], creating a converged, integrated regulatory authority for the communications industry.

Electronic Communications Transactions

Under the Electronic Communications and Transaction Act, BOCRA has responsibility for facilitating e-commerce frameworks and digital transaction governance in Botswana.

Learn More →

The CRA Act mandates BOCRA to regulate all broadcasting; subscription management services and re-broadcasting activities, save for state broadcasting. BOCRA regulates commercial stations including Yarona FM, Duma FM, Gabz FM, and eBotswana.

Commercial radio stations are available in most major towns and villages, with online broadcasting extending access worldwide. eBotswana television is available in Gaborone and within a 60km radius through terrestrial broadcasting, with satellite coverage planned.

Broadcasters are required to promote music tracks by local artists, with licences specifying a minimum percentage of local content to comply with.

Learn More about Broadcasting →

The CRA Act, 2012 ushered in a new era of regulation for the postal sector as BOCRA assumed the mandate of supervising the provision of postal services in Botswana. The Act prohibits any person from providing postal services without a valid BOCRA licence.

BOCRA is mandated to ensure the provision of safe, reliable, efficient and affordable postal services throughout Botswana. Regulatory instruments include the Postal Sector Licensing Framework, which guides the provision of postal services and supports innovation in the sector.

Type Approval

BOCRA is mandated by Sec 84 of the CRA Act to type approve communications equipment connected, used or operated to provide broadcasting or telecommunications services in Botswana.

BOCRA is responsible for planning and managing the national radio frequency spectrum in accordance with the CRA Act and ITU Region 1 guidelines.

National Radio Frequency Plan
