Mandate

The Botswana Communications Regulatory Authority (BOCRA) is an independent communications regulatory authority established through the Communications Regulatory Authority Act 2012 (CRA) on 1 April 2013.

BOCRA's mandate is to regulate the communications sector in Botswana comprising:

  • Telecommunications
  • Internet and Information and Communications Technologies (ICTs)
  • Radio communications
  • Broadcasting
  • Postal services
  • Related matters

Functions of BOCRA

  • Issue licences for the provision of communications services
  • Regulate telecommunications, broadcasting, postal and radio communications
  • Promote competition in the communications sector
  • Protect consumers of communications services
  • Manage the radio frequency spectrum
  • Type approve communications equipment
  • Promote universal access to communications services
